# AI Coffee

AI Coffee is a monthly live meetup that turns AI curiosity into concrete, demonstrated skill — currently in concept-validation, run by a two-person team (Michael + Xavier).

## Language

**Session**:
One monthly live episode (currently ~1h30, online via Google Meet), numbered sequentially (`sessions/01.md`, `sessions/02.md`, ...). Each session's `.md` file is the content source used to generate its slide deck — it is written in finished, narrative form (titles, talk summaries) as soon as the content is planned, which may be *before* the session actually happens. The past tense in a session file does not imply the session has occurred yet — check the event calendar for the real date.
_Avoid_: Episode (used loosely in deck copy, but "Session" is the canonical term in planning docs)

**Cohort cap**:
The intentional ceiling of 12 attendees per online session, chosen to preserve small, high-interaction intimacy. It is a deliberate choice "for now," not a platform limitation — it may be lifted once the format is proven and/or sessions move physical/hybrid.
_Avoid_: Capacity limit (implies a technical constraint, which this isn't)

**Le Ring**:
Deprecated. Originally planned as a 40-minute live, whole-room co-building phase ("on part d'un besoin de la salle, on construit, on mesure"). Dropped in favor of pre-built interactive demos during the session, with personalized case work moved to **Prompt Bar** instead. Do not describe current sessions as including Le Ring.
_Avoid_: Co-construction phase

**Prompt Bar**:
A planned signature offering: 1:1 micro-consulting where an attendee brings their own use case and it gets prototyped together with them ("Venez avec votre cas, on prototype ensemble"). Sits *below* `A Coffee for Teams` (it's individual-facing, not company-wide). Paid/B2B micro-consulting in eventual intent, but for 2026 it is design + soft-launch only (announced inside a regular monthly session, no committed paying client this year).
_Avoid_: Le Ring, co-building (this replaces that function, but is a separate, narrower product)

**Use Case Catalog**:
A planned signature offering: a public catalog of use cases where a prospect can pick one that resonates and have it customized/implemented for them. Distinct from `Prompt Bar` (catalog-driven productization vs. open-ended 1:1 discovery) and from the doc's general "librairie de cas d'usage" (catalog is the productized, paid evolution of that library, not the library itself). Not committed for 2026 — design/seeding only, if at all.
_Avoid_: Case library (the library is the free/public showcase; the catalog is the paid productization built on top of it)

**A Coffee for Teams**:
The largest B2B offering: privatizing the whole meetup format for a single company (content adapted to their business, demos on their own data). Sits *above* `Prompt Bar` and `Use Case Catalog` in scope and price. Out of scope for 2026 — Q4 stretch territory at most, not a committed deliverable.
_Avoid_: B2B program (too vague — there are now three distinct B2B-adjacent offerings at different scales)

**Concept validation phase**:
The current stage of the project (as of mid-2026): pre-Founding-Members, pre-website, pre-revenue, single-digit-to-low-double-digit attendance, monthly cadence with two co-organizers. Phase 1 ("Amorçage communautaire") in the original plan, but at a much smaller scale than that plan's metrics assumed.

**Co-organizer**:
Michael and Xavier share planning, content prep, and hosting equally. This is not "founder + future hire" — both are present from session #2 onward. Use this term, not "porteur seul" (solo founder), when describing current team risk/capacity.
